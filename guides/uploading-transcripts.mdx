## Overview

Sift automatically extracts course information from your academic transcript PDF using AI-powered parsing. This guide walks you through uploading your transcript and troubleshooting common issues.

<Note>
Sift accepts only PDF files up to 2MB in size. Most university transcripts are under 500KB and will upload without issues.
</Note>

## Uploading Your Transcript

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your transcript PDF">
    Download an official or unofficial transcript from your university's student portal. Ensure the file is in PDF format.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload the file">
    Navigate to your Sift dashboard and click the "Upload Transcript" button. Select your PDF file when prompted.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for parsing">
    Sift uses Claude AI to extract course information from your transcript. This typically takes 5-15 seconds depending on transcript length.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review extracted courses">
    After parsing completes, review the list of extracted courses. Each course will include:
    - **Course code** (e.g., "CSCE 121")
    - **Course name** (e.g., "Introduction to Program Design and Concepts")
    - **Credit hours** (0-6 credits)
    - **Letter grade** (A, B, C, D, F, W, IP, etc.)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Accepted File Types

Sift only accepts **PDF files** with the following requirements:

- **File format:** PDF (`.pdf` extension)
- **File size:** Maximum 2MB
- **MIME type:** `application/pdf`
- **Content:** Must contain readable text (not scanned images without OCR)

<Warning>
Files must be valid PDFs starting with the PDF magic bytes (`%PDF`). Renamed files or corrupted PDFs will be rejected with an error message.
</Warning>

## How Course Extraction Works

Sift uses a multi-step process to extract courses from your transcript:

1. **PDF Validation:** The file is checked for valid PDF structure and size limits
2. **Text Extraction:** Text content is extracted from the PDF (up to 50,000 characters)
3. **AI Parsing:** Claude Haiku analyzes the text and identifies course records
4. **Validation:** Extracted courses are validated against expected formats:
   - Course codes must match pattern: 2-5 uppercase letters + space + 3 digits (e.g., `CSCE 121`, `MATH 151A`)
   - Credits must be between 0 and 6
   - Grades must be valid: A, B, C, D, F, W, IP, I, S, U, Q, CR, NC
5. **Sanitization:** Course names are trimmed to 200 characters and normalized

<Tip>
Courses with grades like "W" (withdrawn) or "IP" (in progress) are still extracted and stored. This helps Sift understand your full academic history.
</Tip>

## Valid Course Codes

Sift recognizes course codes that follow this pattern:

- 2-5 uppercase letters for the department (e.g., `CSCE`, `MATH`, `ENGL`)
- A space
- 3 digits for the course number (e.g., `121`, `151`, `304`)
- An optional letter suffix (e.g., `151A`, `304H`)

**Valid examples:**
- `CSCE 121`
- `MATH 151A`
- `ENGL 104`
- `PSYCH 107`

**Invalid examples:**
- `CS121` (missing space)
- `CSCE-121` (hyphen instead of space)
- `CSCE 1210` (too many digits)

## Valid Grades

The following letter grades are recognized:

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|----------|
| A, B, C, D, F | Standard letter grades |
| W | Withdrawn |
| IP | In Progress |
| I | Incomplete |
| S / U | Satisfactory / Unsatisfactory |
| Q | Dropped |
| CR / NC | Credit / No Credit |

<Note>
If a grade doesn't match the expected values, it will be recorded as `?` (unknown).
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Error: 'Only PDF files are accepted'">
  Make sure your file has a `.pdf` extension and is genuinely a PDF file. Simply renaming a `.docx` or `.jpg` file to `.pdf` will not work.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Error: 'File does not appear to be a valid PDF'">
  The uploaded file failed PDF validation. This happens when:
  - The file is corrupted
  - The file was renamed from another format
  - The file doesn't start with the PDF magic bytes
  
  Try downloading your transcript again from your university portal.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Error: 'Could not extract text from PDF'">
  The PDF uploaded successfully, but Sift couldn't extract readable text. This usually means:
  - Your transcript is a scanned image without OCR (Optical Character Recognition)
  - The PDF is encrypted or password-protected
  - The PDF contains less than 20 characters of text
  
  Try requesting a text-based transcript from your university or use an OCR tool to convert scanned transcripts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Error: 'Failed to parse transcript data'">
  The text was extracted, but the AI couldn't identify course records in the expected format. This may happen if:
  - Your transcript uses a non-standard format
  - The document is not actually a transcript
  - The transcript is in a language other than English
  
  Contact support and we can help manually parse your transcript.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Some courses are missing after upload">
  Sift applies strict validation rules during extraction. Courses may be filtered out if:
  - The course code doesn't match the expected pattern
  - Credits are listed as negative or greater than 6
  - The grade is not in the recognized list
  
  You can manually add missing courses through the course management interface.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Course names are truncated">
  Course names longer than 200 characters are automatically truncated to prevent display issues. The full name is typically not needed for course identification.
</Accordion>

## What Happens Next

Once your transcript is successfully parsed:

1. **Courses are saved** to your user profile as completed courses
2. **Degree requirements** are automatically calculated based on your completed courses
3. **Recommendations** are generated based on remaining requirements and your preferences
4. You can **customize your preferences** to adjust how courses are scored and ranked

<Tip>
After uploading your transcript, head to the [Customizing Preferences](/guides/customizing-preferences) guide to personalize your course recommendations.
</Tip>
